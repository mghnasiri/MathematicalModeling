Economic Order Quantity

EOQ — Classic, Backorder, Quantity Discounts — 18 Tests

The foundational inventory model determining the optimal order quantity that minimizes total holding and ordering costs. Includes extensions for planned backorders (allowing stockouts with a penalty cost) and quantity discounts (all-units and incremental).

Q* = sqrt(2*D*K / h)
TC(Q) = D*K/Q + h*Q/2
where D = demand rate, K = order cost, h = holding cost
AlgorithmTypeComplexityDetails
EOQ Formula Exact O(1) Classic Harris-Wilson formula; backorder & discount extensions

Dynamic Lot Sizing

Silver-Meal & Wagner-Whitin Heuristics — 18 Tests

Determine production/order quantities over a finite planning horizon with time-varying demand. Silver-Meal minimizes average cost per period; the Least Unit Cost and Part Period Balancing heuristics provide alternatives for different demand patterns.

AlgorithmTypeComplexityDetails
Wagner-Whitin DP Exact O(T²) Dynamic programming; optimal for uncapacitated lot sizing
Silver-Meal Heuristic O(T) Minimize average cost per period; excellent for lumpy demand

Wagner-Whitin Algorithm

Exact Dynamic Programming — 13 Tests

The optimal dynamic programming solution for the uncapacitated single-item lot sizing problem. Exploits the zero-inventory ordering property to achieve O(T^2) complexity over T periods. Provides the gold standard against which heuristic lot sizing methods are benchmarked.

f(t) = min_{1 ≤ s ≤ t} { f(s-1) + K + h * ∑_{k=s}^{t} (k-s)*d_k }
Zero-inventory property: order only when inventory = 0
AlgorithmTypeComplexityDetails
Wagner-Whitin DP Exact O(T²) Optimal forward DP exploiting zero-inventory ordering (Wagner & Whitin, 1958)

Capacitated Lot Sizing

CLSP — 31 Tests

Extension of dynamic lot sizing with production capacity constraints per period. The capacity constraint makes the problem NP-hard, requiring heuristic approaches. Models realistic manufacturing settings where production resources are limited.

AlgorithmTypeComplexityDetails
Greedy CLSP Heuristic O(T²) Greedy forward pass with capacity-aware lot merging
Greedy CLS Heuristic O(T²) Cost-balancing heuristic respecting capacity constraints

Multi-Echelon Inventory

Serial & Distribution Systems — 17 Tests

Inventory management across multiple levels of a supply chain (e.g., warehouse to distribution center to retail). Optimizes base-stock levels considering lead times, demand variability, and inter-echelon dependencies for system-wide cost minimization.

AlgorithmTypeDetails
Base Stock Policy Heuristic Echelon base-stock levels with service level targets
Greedy Allocation Heuristic Marginal cost allocation across echelons

Safety Stock Optimization

Analytical & Simulation-Based — 14 Tests

Determine optimal safety stock levels to buffer against demand and supply uncertainty while meeting target service levels. Covers continuous review (Q, R) and periodic review (s, S) policies with normal and empirical demand distributions.

SS = z_alpha * sigma_D * sqrt(L)
where z_alpha = service level z-score, sigma_D = demand std, L = lead time
AlgorithmTypeComplexityDetails
Analytical Safety Stock Exact O(1) Closed-form formulas for normal demand; cycle/fill service levels
