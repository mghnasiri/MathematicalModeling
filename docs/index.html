Astronomy & Space
Astronomy — Spacecraft Operations

James Webb Space Telescope Scheduling

Scheduling · Multi-Objective + 3D Vector Resource

JWST's observation queue is a multi-objective scheduling problem over 3D vector resources (power, data, thermal). NASA/STScI's Spike planner builds feasible long-range plans for 10,000+ proposals per cycle, maximising science return while respecting slew, momentum-dump, and thermal constraints.

Operational since 2022 Explore →
Space — Interplanetary Mission Design

Gravity-Assist Trajectory Sequence Design

Combinatorial Optimisation · NP-Hard (proven)

Designing a sequence of planetary flybys to reach a distant target (e.g., Saturn via Venus-Venus-Earth-Jupiter) is a combinatorial explosion over flyby bodies, dates, and Δv budgets. ESA's Rosetta mission explored >106 trajectory trees. Branch-and-prune plus Lambert solvers are standard.

Astronomy — Radio Telescope Arrays

Radio Telescope Array Configuration (VLA / SKA / ngEHT)

Facility Location · Multi-Objective Coverage

Placing antenna dishes across kilometres of terrain to maximise uv-plane coverage is a facility-location problem. The SKA project optimised 197 dish positions across South Africa and Australia using multi-objective evolutionary algorithms to balance sensitivity, resolution, and side-lobe suppression.

Published Research Explore →
Space — Space Traffic Management

Space Debris Collision Avoidance

Stochastic Optimisation · POMDP

With 30,000+ tracked objects in LEO, deciding whether to manoeuvre a satellite is a POMDP over uncertain conjunction data. ESA's CrashWatch and NASA's CARA system process ~1 million conjunction warnings/year, optimising manoeuvre cost against collision probability under state uncertainty.

Genomics & Bioinformatics
Genomics — Sequence Assembly

Genome Assembly — de novo Sequencing

Shortest Common Superstring · Hamiltonian / Eulerian Path · NP-Hard (proven x3)

Reconstructing a genome from millions of short reads is NP-hard under all three major graph paradigms — overlap (Hamiltonian path), de Bruijn (Eulerian path), and string graph — each proved formally. Pevzner et al. (2001) reformulated the problem as Eulerian path traversal, enabling the Human Genome Project. Tools SPAdes, Hifiasm, and Verkko are operational worldwide.

Proven + Operational Explore →
Bioinformatics — Sequence Analysis

Multiple Sequence Alignment

NP-Hard Combinatorial Alignment · Dynamic Programming · Profile HMMs

Aligning three or more biological sequences to reveal evolutionary relationships was proved NP-hard by Wang & Jiang (1994). ClustalW, MUSCLE, and T-Coffee use progressive heuristics; HMMER uses profile HMMs. These tools underpin virtually all comparative genomics and phylogenetics research worldwide.

Operational Explore →
Structural Biology — Protein Design

Protein Side-Chain Packing

NP-Hard Combinatorial Optimisation · ILP · Dead-End Elimination

Given a protein backbone, choosing rotamer conformations for each residue to minimise total energy was proved NP-hard by Pierce & Winfree (2002). Rosetta's packer uses Monte Carlo + dead-end elimination. This powers computational protein design including the 2024 Nobel Prize-winning work on de novo proteins.

Operational Explore →
Ecology & Conservation
Conservation Biology — Reserve Design

Conservation Reserve Network Design (Marxan)

Minimum-Cost Set Covering · 0-1 Integer Programme · Simulated Annealing

Selecting protected areas to cover all species at minimum cost is minimum-cost set covering (NP-hard). Marxan, used in 100+ countries, applies simulated annealing to find near-optimal reserve networks. It designed the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park rezoning protecting 33% of reef area.

Conservation Biology — Species Prioritisation

Biodiversity Spatial Prioritisation (Zonation / Prioritizr)

Maximum Coverage · Weighted Set Cover · ILP

Prioritising land for conservation to maximise species representation under budget constraints is maximum coverage. Zonation uses cell-removal heuristics; Prioritizr solves exact ILPs. Both support the global 30×30 target (protect 30% of land/sea by 2030).

Wildlife Corridor Design

Steiner Tree Problem · Minimum Cost Network Design · Circuit Theory

Connecting fragmented habitats with corridors minimising cost while ensuring species movement is a Steiner tree / minimum-cost network design problem. Circuitscape models landscape connectivity as electrical circuits. Used for grizzly bear, jaguar, and tiger corridor planning across continents.

Climate Model Ensemble Selection

p-Dispersion · Maximum Diversity · Multi-Criteria Optimisation

Selecting a representative subset from 50+ CMIP6 climate models is a p-dispersion / maximum diversity problem. The IPCC uses ensemble selection to span the uncertainty space while keeping computational costs tractable for regional impact studies and national adaptation planning.

Earth Observation — Sensor Networks

Environmental Sensor Placement (Argo / GCOS)

Facility Location · Submodular Maximisation · (1-1/e) Guarantee

Placing ocean sensors to maximise information gain is submodular maximisation — a facility location variant with provable (1-1/e) approximation guarantee. The Argo programme deployed 4,000 profiling floats worldwide. Krause et al. (2008) showed greedy placement near-optimal for environmental monitoring.

Wildfire Suppression Resource Dispatch

Stochastic Multi-Resource Assignment · MIP under Uncertainty

Dispatching firefighting crews, air tankers, and engines to wildfires under uncertain spread is stochastic multi-resource assignment. The US Forest Service uses MIP-based dispatch systems. Donovan & Rideout (2003) formulated the foundational model used across North American fire agencies.

Neuroscience
Network Neuroscience — Connectomics

Brain Connectome Community Detection

Modularity Maximisation · NP-Hard Graph Clustering · Louvain / D-Wave QUBO

Partitioning the brain's structural or functional connectome into communities is modularity maximisation — proved NP-hard by Brandes et al. (2007). The Louvain algorithm is standard in neuroscience; Crimi et al. (2023) demonstrated D-Wave quantum annealing on connectome QUBO formulations.

Neuroimaging — Source Localisation

EEG / MEG Brain Source Localisation

L1-Regularised Optimisation · Basis Pursuit / LASSO · Underdetermined Inverse Problem

Localising neural sources from scalp EEG/MEG measurements is an underdetermined inverse problem solved via L1-regularised optimisation (basis pursuit / LASSO). LORETA and MNE-Python implement these methods. Used clinically for pre-surgical epilepsy mapping and in cognitive neuroscience research worldwide.

Cognitive Neuroscience — Experimental Design

fMRI Stimulus Sequence Optimisation

Combinatorial Sequence Design · Statistical Efficiency Maximisation

Designing the order and timing of stimuli in fMRI experiments to maximise statistical detection power is combinatorial sequence optimisation. Tools like optseq2 (FreeSurfer) use rapid event-related designs with jittered ISIs. Used in thousands of neuroimaging studies to optimise experimental efficiency.

Physics & Materials
Materials Science — Structure Prediction

Crystal Structure Prediction (USPEX / AIRSS)

Global Metaheuristic Search · Evolutionary Algorithm · Energy Landscape

Predicting stable crystal structures from chemical composition alone is global optimisation over a rugged energy landscape. USPEX uses evolutionary algorithms; AIRSS uses random search with DFT relaxation. Predicted the high-temperature superconductor LaH₁₀ (confirmed at 250K, 170 GPa) before experimental synthesis.

Quantum Physics — Hardware Architecture

Ising Spin Glass → D-Wave Quantum Annealer

QUBO · Proven Equivalence to All NP-Hard Problems (Lucas 2014)

Lucas (2014) proved that every NP-hard problem can be mapped to a QUBO / Ising model, making quantum annealers universal NP-hard solvers in principle. D-Wave Advantage (5,000+ qubits) implements this natively. Applications span logistics, finance, drug discovery, and materials science.

Operational — D-Wave Advantage Explore →
Particle Physics — Facility Scheduling

Particle Accelerator Beam-Time Scheduling

RCPSP · Parallel Machine Scheduling · Shared Renewable Resource

Scheduling experiments on shared beamlines at facilities like CERN LHC and ESRF is RCPSP with renewable resources (beam intensity, detector time, cryogenics). Hundreds of proposals compete for limited beam-time each cycle. Constraint programming and priority-based heuristics are used operationally.

Computational Chemistry
Computational Chemistry — Synthesis Planning

Retrosynthetic Pathway Planning (AiZynthFinder / ASKCOS)

AND-OR Tree Search · Monte Carlo Tree Search · Shortest Path in Reaction Space

Planning a multi-step synthesis route from target molecule to available reagents is AND-OR tree search over reaction space. Pioneered by Corey & Wipke (1969, Nobel Prize). AiZynthFinder (AstraZeneca) and ASKCOS (MIT) use MCTS with neural network expansion policies. Deployed across pharmaceutical R&D.

Operational — Pharmaceutical industry Explore →
Computational Chemistry — Material Discovery

High-Throughput Catalyst Screening (Bayesian Optimisation)

Bayesian Optimisation · Sequential Multi-Armed Bandit · Gaussian Process Surrogate

Screening thousands of potential catalysts to find optimal reaction conditions is Bayesian optimisation with Gaussian process surrogates. Shields et al. (2021, Nature) demonstrated 100x speedup over random screening. Deployed at pharmaceutical and chemical companies for reaction optimisation.

Operational — Industry deployed Explore →
Computational Chemistry — Drug Discovery

Molecular Docking / Virtual Drug Screening

Branch-and-Bound over Torsion Space · Combinatorial Configuration Search

Predicting how a drug molecule binds to a protein target is branch-and-bound over torsion angles in 3D space. AutoDock Vina searches billions of configurations using iterated local search. Virtual screening is standard at Pfizer, Roche, and Novartis, filtering millions of compounds before wet-lab testing.

Mathematics & Theory
Mathematics — Theorem Proving

Four Color Theorem — First Computer-Verified Proof (1976)

Graph Coloring · NP-Hard · Exhaustive Computational Verification

Appel & Haken (1976) proved that any planar map can be coloured with at most 4 colours by reducing to 1,936 configurations and computationally verifying each — the first major theorem proved by computer. General graph colouring remains NP-hard; this proof verified a special case exhaustively.

Proven — Mathematical theorem Explore →
Mathematics — Geometry

Kepler Conjecture — 150,000+ LP Relaxations

Linear Programming · 150,000+ Instances · Formal Verification (Flyspeck)

Hales (1998) proved the optimal sphere packing density (π/3√2) by solving 150,000+ LP relaxations computationally. The proof was so complex that referees could only be "99% certain" — leading to the Flyspeck project which formally verified the entire proof in Isabelle/HOL and HOL Light (2014).

Proven — 150,000+ LP runs Explore →
Mathematics — Combinatorics

Ramsey Number Bounding via SAT / ILP

Integer Programming · SAT Solving · Exhaustive Branch-and-Bound

Computing Ramsey numbers R(s,t) — the minimum graph size guaranteeing a monochromatic clique — requires exhaustive combinatorial search. McKay & Radziszowski (1995) proved R(4,5)=25 using massive computation. R(5,5) remains unknown despite decades of effort — Erdős famously said aliens would demand it as ransom.
